Step by Step

Step 1

Find a desk or level surface so that you can sit/stand close to your laptop.

Step 2

Use a separate keyboard and mouse with your laptop.

Step 3

Position the keyboard and mouse directly in front of you within easy reach.

Step 4

Position your laptop so that the top of the screen is level with your eye height. If you don’t have a laptop riser, use a box file or some books to raise your laptop.

Step 5

If sitting, use an adjustable chair. Adjust your chair height so that you can use your keyboard and mouse with your wrists straight and your forearms horizontal. Use a rolled up item of clothing for extra comfort/support.

Step 6

SITTING – Maintain a good posture whilst sitting, try to ensure that the small of your back is supported, your shoulders are relaxed (not slumped, not elevated), and that there is no unwelcome pressure on the back of your knees.

Step 7

STANDING – Maintain a good posture whilst standing, keep your legs, torso, neck and head approximately in line and vertical – don’t slouch, lean or twist to the side.

Step 8

Don’t sit or stand for too long – change your posture every few minutes and take regular micro-breaks away from your laptop.

Step 9

Use collaboration spaces etc. for short durations only. If you plan to carry out intensive or prolonged computer work, use a suitable flexible working desk.
