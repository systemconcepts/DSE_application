Question 1

Can you adjust your desk height so that it is around elbow height, with your shoulders relaxed?

Information

If you are unable to adjust your sit-to-stand desk to a suitable height or you are unsure how to do it, contact your Workplace Co-ordinator.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.

Question 2

Are your wrists and hands in a straight and comfortable position when typing or using the mouse?

Information

Adjust the keyboard tilt to help you find a comfortable position.  Try to use a soft touch on the keys and don't overstretch your fingers.

Avoid leaving your hand(s) on the keyboard/mouse when you’re not using it.  If you don’t feel like you can obtain a comfortable position, you may benefit from using a wrist rest for support.

your Workplace Co-ordinator will be able to advise on how to obtain one or in London email Stationery Purchasing.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.

Question 3

Are your shoulders and arms relaxed comfortably at your side, not outstretched, while using your keyboard and/or mouse?

Information

Position your keyboard and mouse directly in front of you, close enough to the desk edge so that you don’t need to stretch to reach them.

This will be easier if you stand upright and close to your desk. There should be space in front of the keyboard (around 10cm or so from the desk edge) so you can rest your hands and wrists when not typing.

Position your mouse next to your keyboard within easy reach.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.

Question 4

Is your monitor distance about an arm’s length away?

Information

Move your monitor closer or further away so that you can see the text/images clearly without straining your eyes.

Adjust the screen brightness, contrast and resolution to make the information easier to see. Have your eyes tested if you are having difficulty seeing the screen.

If you use multiple monitors and you use one more than the other, centre the primary screen in front of you and place the secondary monitor on either side of the primary monitor and angle the outer edge towards you.

If you use both monitors equally, they should be the positioned at the same height, centred in front of your body and angled inwards slightly.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.

Question 5

Is the top of your monitor at or slightly below eye level?

Information

If you use a laptop and your screen is too low, use a separate keyboard and mouse, and either connect your laptop to an external screen or use an adjustable laptop stand.

If you use a desktop and the screen is too low, you will need something to lift it to the correct height. Use screen risers to do this (your Workplace Co-ordinator can advise on how to obtain one of these or in London email Stationery Purchasing) or place the screen on anything that is suitable and stable, like box files or packs of paper.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.

Question 6

Do you take a 2-3 minute stretch/movement break away from your workstation every hour or so?

Information

Try to move away from your desk when there is a natural break in your work. Stretching and movement breaks are recommended every hour or so.

Go to speak to a colleague in person rather than emailing or phoning them. Break up long spells of computer working with tasks requiring movement, e.g. printing, filing etc.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.

Question 7

Do you feel comfortable when using your computer workstation?

Information

You must report any symptoms that you think may be caused or made worse by using your computer or workstation to the ergonomics team.

Symptoms to report include headaches, irritated eyes, difficulties reading screen/documents, and tingling, numbness or discomfort in the back, neck, arms or wrists, hands or fingers, feet or legs.

Please press the ‘Help’ button to email an ergonomist.

Click ‘Continue to next question’ or Click ‘Back’ to exit the questionnaire.
