Step by Step

Step 1

Try and sit so that you can use your laptop with your wrists straight and your forearms horizontal. If your chair is too low, sit on your coat to raise your seat height.

Step 2

Use a separate keyboard and mouse with your laptop.

Step 3

Position the laptop/keyboard and mouse so you can reach it comfortably, without leaning or reaching forwards.

Step 4

Position your laptop so that the top of the screen is level with your eye height. If you don’t have a laptop riser, use a a bag or some books to raise your laptop.

Step 5

Avoid resting your arms on the edge of the desk/table, particularly if it has a 90° edge, as high pressure on your forearm can cause discomfort.

Step 6

Use a cushion, or rolled up item of clothing, to support the small of your back if the chair does not offer appropriate support.

Step 7

Carry as little as possible when travelling. Leave any unnecessary items in the office or at home and keep as much information as possible electronically, rather than on paper.

Step 8

Don’t sit for too long – change your posture every few minutes and take regular micro-breaks away from your laptop.
